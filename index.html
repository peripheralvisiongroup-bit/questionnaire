Research Context

Peripheral vision is crucial for complex decision-making in dynamic environments, from driving during rush hour to team sports competition. While research has identified key factors affecting peripheral perception (crowding, eccentricity, predictions, dual tasks), there remains a critical gap: we lack tools to measure how athletes subjectively rate their usage of peripheral vision in real-world performance contexts. This project bridges basic and applied science by developing assessment tools that work in authentic sport environments.

Research Focus

Athletes in team sports rely heavily on peripheral vision to track teammates, opponents, and the ball simultaneously. Yet we lack reliable subjective measures to complement objective performance indices. This research aims to bridge that gap. This project is part of a larger initiative with aim to better understand mechanisms of peripheral vision in complex decision making (SNF Grant number 211220).

What You'll Develop:

Working from an existing database of approximately 70 statements related to peripheral vision mechanisms, you will:

  • Conduct validation studies with athletes ranging from amateur to 1st league level
  • Pilot test the instrument in real-world team sport contexts (primarily football and basketball)
