Research Context

In many sports, but also in everyday tasks such as driving, it is often essential to perceive several pieces of information at the same time and to make decisions under time pressure. Our team will investigate when we process information without looking at it directly. This is achieved with "peripheral perception", whose role in complex decision-making situations has hardly been investigated to date. In the first part of the project, we will manipulate real football situations in virtual reality in such a way that we can examine the role of peripheral perception based on decision-making and gaze behavior. These results will be used in the second part to develop peripheral perception training for sports practice. The third part will examine whether the findings can also be transferred to car drivers and whether they can perceive other road users better and faster with certain perception strategies.

Research Focus

Athletes in team sports rely heavily on peripheral vision to track teammates, opponents, and the ball simultaneously. Yet we lack reliable subjective measures to complement objective performance indices. This research aims to bridge that gap. This project is part of a larger initiative with aim to better understand mechanisms of peripheral vision in complex decision making (SNF Grant number 211220).

What You'll Develop:

Working from an existing database of approximately 70 statements related to peripheral vision mechanisms, you will:

  • Refine and validate a questionnaire measuring peripheral vision awareness in team sport athletes
  • Conduct validation studies with athletes ranging from amateur to 1st league level
  • Pilot test the instrument in real-world team sport contexts (primarily football and basketball)
